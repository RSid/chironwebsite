Team

Eric Ingersoll (CEO) has recruited many highly talented teams, and built them into high-performing organizations. He raised over $100 million of private equity for General Compression, his last company, of which he was a founder and the lead inventor of the technology. He has negotiated strategic partnerships and investments with companies and utilities in the US, UK, Europe, China and Japan.

He has also been an interim leader or paid strategic advisor to over 30 startups, many in the energy industry. He has also worked for private foundations such as the Rockefeller Brothers Fund, the MacArthur Foundation, The Surdna Foundation, and others, as well as innovative programs at the World Bank, the IFC, the US Department of Energy, NREL, USEPA, the California Energy Commission and the Massachusetts Renewable Energy Trust. He has a unique strategic vision and wealth of experience developing/commercializing new technologies. Eric was born in London, spent his childhood in West Africa, and currently resides in Cambridge, MA.

Edgar Roberts (Project Manager) is a talented and experienced engineering and construction project manager. He has supervised multimillion dollar construction and dismantling projects of Q-BOP facilities, designed and operated waste recycling plants, and developed equipment for worldwide use including:
  • A new gas cleaning system.
  • The design criteria for a high efficiency hydrochloric acid removal system.
  • An “on-line” attrition system to reduce the agglomerate size of iron oxide particles.
  • Modifications for existing pumps for highly corrosive fluids increasing their life between 4 and 8 times.
  • A new spray nozzle system designed to obtain extremely fine iron oxide particles.
  • A new design concept for the waste injection system that led to a life increase of the components.
He has also managed a company in Argentina for more than 13 years, which was profitable from the first day of operation.

Vladimir Knobel (Chief Engineer) is a highly skilled and industrious engineer with comprehensive technical expertise in mechanical/design engineering, equipment maintenance, safety, project management, manufacturing, field support, and vendor management. He has generated conceptual design and detail drawings for ASME pressure vessels, inside pressure vessels parts, connections for instruments for process control, piping, test fixtures, and special attachments. He has also designed and detailed drawings for increased efficiency of plant equipment, including arc vacuum furnaces, reactors, crushers, tooling for presses, rolling mills, and wire producing machines.

He designed improvements to new/existing material handling equipment for production as well as safe operation, including pushing mechanism for inserting heavy bars into rolls and rotating device. He also developed new apron, spring protective mechanism, and roll polishing fixture at production mill. Knobel held responsibility for holding material fixtures for a sulfuric acid tank as well as clamping and movable table for dies for 2,000-/1,500-ton presses. He has designed automated robotic machine systems, including fixture handling, processing, and part cleaning for manufacturer of metal finishing equipment.
